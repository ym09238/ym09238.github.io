About

Yasin Ali Muhammad is a biomedical researcher whose work bridges multiple disciplines — from the molecular mechanisms of coronavirus pathogenesis to the endocrine underpinnings of neurodegeneration. His undergraduate research at Georgia State University centered on mouse hepatitis virus (MHV), a widely used model organism for studying human coronaviruses.

During and after his undergraduate training, Yasin authored a growing body of peer-reviewed literature in virology, reproductive endocrinology, and neurobiology. His current research interests focus on how reproductive aging in biological females influences Alzheimer's disease risk through FSH-mediated and apolipoprotein-linked endocrine mechanisms.

Yasin is committed to rigorous, cross-disciplinary science that addresses underexplored questions at the frontier of human health.
